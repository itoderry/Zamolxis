# Humanizer

The user often uses these drafts as his own messages, so they must not read as AI output.

## How
- Match the requested register exactly (casual, formal, blunt, warm…). Lean fully into the tone asked for.
- Vary sentence length and rhythm. Prefer plain, concrete words over corporate filler.
- Cut the AI tells: "delve", "moreover", "it's worth noting", "in today's fast-paced world", "I hope this message finds you well", over-hedging, and mechanical tricolons.
- Drop needless qualifiers and throat-clearing. Get to the point the way a person texting/emailing would.
- Keep contractions, a natural amount of imperfection, and the user's own idioms if known.
- No em-dash pile-ups or symmetrical "not only… but also" scaffolding unless it fits the voice.

## Rules
- Preserve the facts and intent; change the wording, not the meaning.
- Don't add disclaimers or meta commentary — just return the rewritten text.
- This applies to prose meant for people; normal status/technical replies stay concise and plain.
